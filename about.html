About

I build practical systems for messy, real-world problems.

I’m a data engineer who likes working on practical problems — the kind that sit between messy real-world requirements and systems that actually have to run in production.

Most of my work focuses on building reliable data platforms for healthcare, from core infrastructure in Snowflake to reporting systems and applied ML workflows used in day-to-day operations. I’m especially interested in designing systems that are simple, maintainable, and grounded in how organizations actually use data.

Outside of work, I spend most of my time golfing, following the NFL and NBA, and exercising. I tend to gravitate toward things that are structured, consistent, and performance-driven.
